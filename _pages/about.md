<h2 id="my-interests">My Interests</h2>
I enjoy and am interested in programming, physics, biology, teaching, and anything that combines them. This directly aligns not only with my academic background, but with my previous appointments as a computer science teaching assistant at University of California, San Diego (UCSD) and current position doing software development as a research assistant at the [Gravitational-Wave Physics and Astronomy Center (GWPAC)](https://physics.fullerton.edu/gwpac/) at California State University, Fullerton (CSUF). Being a teaching assistant at UCSD was my most precious time spent as a graduate student, and contributing to gravitational-wave physics research is the most exciting thing I have done. You can learn more about these on my <a href="../experience">Experience</a> and <a href="../publications">Publications</a> pages.

I also enjoy mentoring and participating in service and outreach. You can learn more about these on my <a href="../service-and-outreach">Service and Outreach</a> page.

<h2 id="my-journey">My Journey</h2>
My academic path is a little more unconventional than most. It details my multidisciplinary background and reflects my interdisciplinary interests in computer science, physics, and biology.

#### UCLA, *undergraduate*
In 2015, I graduated from University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) with a B.S. in Molecular, Cell, and Developmental Biology. I enjoy most all STEM subjects, but computer science was the one discipline that was still completely foreign to me at the time. For this reason and because I love learning, during my last quarter at UCLA, I took UCLA's introductory C++ course to help demystify the subject. I couldn't have guessed just how much I would enjoy that class, or that the singular decision I made to take it was pivotal in changing my life's path.

#### CSUF, *post-graduate*
After some time off of school after graduating from UCLA, I returned to school with the goal of educating myself in order to apply for and enroll in a masters program in computer science. To this end, I spent two years taking courses in math, computer science, and physics at California State Univeristy, Fullerton (CSUF). During this time, I took the CSUF course, Computational Physics (PHYS 315), which was co-taught by my future research advisor, [Prof. Joshua Smith](https://physics.fullerton.edu/~josmith/). It was due to this course that I met Prof. Smith and joined the [Gravitational-Wave Physics and Astronomy Center (GWPAC)](https://physics.fullerton.edu/gwpac/).

#### UCSD, *graduate*
After two years of courses at CSUF, in 2018 I enrolled in the Computer Science M.S. program at University of California, San Diego (UCSD). During the academic quarters at UCSD, I worked as a teaching assistant in the Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) department, and during the summers I worked at GWPAC doing software development for gravitational-wave physics research efforts. Currently, I work at GWPAC and am set to graduate with a masters in computer science (with a specialization in artificial intelligence) from UCSD in December 2020.
