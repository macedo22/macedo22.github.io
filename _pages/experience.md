<h2 id="research-assistant">Research Assistant, <i>CSUF</i></h2>
Nov 2017 - Sep 2018, Jun - Aug 2019, Jun 2020 - present

I currently work as a research assistant at California State University, Fullerton (CSUF) at the [Gravitational-Wave Physics and Astronomy Center (GWPAC)](https://physics.fullerton.edu/gwpac/). Here, I contribute to the development of software for graviational-wave physics research efforts, in collaboration with the [LIGO Scientific Collaboration (LSC)](https://www.ligo.org/) and [Simulating eXtreme Spacetimes (SXS)](https://www.black-holes.org/).

Previously, I worked with [Prof. Joshua Smith](https://physics.fullerton.edu/~josmith/), where I contributed to and automated software that applies machine learning techniques to [Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory's (LIGO)](https://www.ligo.caltech.edu/) data to identify sources of noise that reduce LIGO's astronomical range. Specifically, I contributed to the [GWDetChar](https://gwdetchar.readthedocs.io/en/stable/) python package and developed an internal automated tool that analyzes noise sources daily (not publicly viewable). I co-authored a paper on some of the gravitational-wave detector characterization research and development of [GWDetChar](https://gwdetchar.readthedocs.io/en/stable/) that I did, published [here](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1361-6382/aae593), and publicly available [here](https://arxiv.org/abs/1807.02592). I also presented on this work at the [34th Pacific Coast Gravity Meeting](http://www.tapir.caltech.edu/~pcgm34/), where the presentation can be found [here](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1Ic5rUPXpaCZA7rVTPqsHnwRKPGYY2yPI/view?usp=sharing).

Currently, I work with [Prof. Geoffrey Lovelace](https://geoffrey-lovelace.com/), where I contribute to the development of [SpECTRE](https://spectre-code.org/), in-development code that uses numerical relativity to simulate and study extreme spacetimes and astrophysical events, including binary black hole and binary neutron star mergers.

<h2 id="teaching-assistant">Teaching Assistant, <i>UCSD</i></h2>
Sep 2018 - Jun 2019, Sep 2019 - Jun 2020

As a graduate student at University of California, San Diego (UCSD), I was a teaching assistant in the department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE). Upon entering UCSD, I received the <strong><u>Golden State Teaching Scholarship</u></strong> from UCSD's CSE department for the 2018 - 2019 academic year, which awarded me my first 3 quarters of teaching assistantships. In Spring 2020, I received the <strong><u>Masters Award for Excellence in Teaching</u></strong> from UCSD's CSE department, which recognizes one masters student for their excellence in and dedication to teaching.

My responsibilites included writing weekly software to automate the grading of homework assignments, leading discussion, hosting office hours, and creating supplementary study and review material.

I was a teaching assistant for 6 academic quarters, for the following courses (course homepages linked where possible):
- [Introduction to Programming in Java I (CSE  8A) Fall 2018](https://ucsd-cse8a-f18.github.io/), *instructed by [Prof. Joe Gibbs Politz](https://jpolitz.github.io/) and [Prof. Mia Minnes](http://cseweb.ucsd.edu/~minnes/index.html)*
- [Basic Data Structures and Object-Oriented Design (CSE 12) Winter 2019](https://ucsd-cse12-w19.github.io/), *instructed by [Prof. Joe Gibbs Politz](https://jpolitz.github.io/)*
- Introduction to Programming in Java I (CSE 8A) Spring 2019, *instructed by [Prof. Leo Porter](https://cseweb.ucsd.edu/~leporter/)*
- Computer Organization and Systems Programming (CSE 30) Fall 2019, *instructed by [Prof. Leo Porter](https://cseweb.ucsd.edu/~leporter/) and [Bryan Chin](https://cse.ucsd.edu/people/faculty-profiles/bryan-chin)*
- [Introduction to Programming in Java I (CSE  8A) Winter 2020](https://ucsd-cse8a-w20.github.io/), *instructed by [Prof. Joe Gibbs Politz](https://jpolitz.github.io/)*
- [Advanced Data Structures (CSE 100) Spring 2020](https://ucsd-cse100-s20.github.io/), *instructed by [Prof. Joe Gibbs Politz](https://jpolitz.github.io/), [Prof. Niema Moshiri](https://niema.net/), and Sander Valstar*
